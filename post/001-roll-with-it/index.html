Roll With It

A set of music for GMTK 2022

 Posted on July 18, 2022

It’s a set of music for the GMTK game jam.

About the game

The theme of this jam is Roll Of The Dice. Our team has created a game consisting of 6 mini-games. Each time the dice is rolled, the player is taken to a different game including:

  • Serve: Serve the right food to the right customer, but do it quickly or they will get angry!
  • Avoid: The goal is simple - avoid the bullets.
  • Jump: Jump over the obstacles and try not to die.
  • Count: Match the number on the screen with the number of dots on the dice before time runs out.
  • Care: Keep Dicey alive by feeding him at the right intervals. Don’t feed him too little - he’ll starve, but don’t feed him too much either.
  • Chill: Take a breather. Maybe pet your cat. Have a sip of tea. This break will not last long, so enjoy it while it lasts.

Music required

Since it consists of 6 mini-games, we decide to make 6 pieces of music. Once for each. Also

  • We are going to make all six pieces of music the same melody, and the same tempo. In this way, we can switch between different games but keep the music playing.
  • However, the 6 mini-games are different in design, the music should fit each mini-game.

Well, to make 6 pieces of music in two days (GMTK 2022 is a 48-hour jam) is not easy. Luckily, we are not going to make 6 different pieces of music, but 1 music and 5 variations.

Design of music

The first music I work on is Serve:

  • Making the melody and harmony is nothing special.
  • It’s a basic binary form music. I try to balance the music in two sections.
  • Choosing the tempo is a BIG headache. It needs to fit all the music (so they are the same length and can switch to each other freely)
  • It’s mostly finished in the afternoon of the first day.

Avoid is the second one:

  • It’s the most challenging game among the six. The player may (or should) be pushed a bit hard.
  • The music should be thrilling but not that thrilling.
  • Some more beats are added so it’s hot-blooded.
  • One more Pad is added so it is thicker.

The third one is the Care:

  • It should be relatively relaxing.
  • As a comparison. Piano and violin are added to make it lush and beautiful (I do love the combination of piano and violin)
  • The String does make it feels a bit slow and calm.
  • Also, the drum sets are changed to a weaker one.
  • We tried to use a meow sound for the melody. It does not work.

Chill is designed for the player to relax:

  • It should be an ambient one.
  • But, sorry, I cannot change the tempo. I try to make it as chill as I could.
  • Large reverb is used. No, a large reveeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeerb is used in the music. It feels good.

Jump comes next:

  • This is as challenging as the Avoid. I’m encouraged to make it overwhelming.
  • A new set of drums is used. To add some force and a punchy feel to it.
  • Personally, the kick fits well in the music like a footstep sound.

The last one, Count:

  • This should be between the Care and Serve. Not as calm as petting the cat, but not that pushing as working in the restaurant.
  • Trumpet and saxophone are used to add a tone of jazz.
  • Drum beats are re-arranged.
  • This is the last minute of the second day. I’m in a hurry. Not sure if it’s well-balanced.
